I scored a 5 on the AP Psychology exam, so yes, I am an AP-level psychologist. I tell everyone this when they need emotional support and a personal therapist.
To answer the question, "How hard is it to be an amateur therapist?" I think the answer would be easier than most people think. I think the better question to ask is, "Are you willing to listen?"
Number one, all useful information on how to be an excellent amateur therapist is in any random YouTube video with "How to... Therapy" in the title. The problem is whether you're really going to listen to its advice to diffuse tricky situations, or if you're going to forget and blow up arguments and dilemmas as a result.
Number two, after you've memorized the YouTube advice, are you really willing to listen to someone's problems? I've found that the best therapeutic sessions I've given were when I strictly listened, with minimal injections of my own input. Giving minimal emotionally charged input is even more important. For example, if someone believes, "The aliens are going to make us into protein noodles with white sauce and eat us!" you should avoid saying, "That's the dumbest thing I've ever heard; that's obviously not going to happen." Instead of making statements, ask good questions so the person you're helping believes they have control and, consequently, feels confident in their answer. It's also an opportunity for reflection. For example, "Why do you think the aliens are going to eat us?"
